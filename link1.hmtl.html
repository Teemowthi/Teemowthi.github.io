Undergraduate Programs
Available Courses Course Description Years of Study
BS COMPUTER SCIENCE Silliman University's College of Computer Studies (CCS) offers a Bachelor of Science (BS) in Computer Science program, focusing on the theoretical foundations of computation, algorithms, and software development to prepare students for careers in fields like software development, information security, and programming. The program is housed in excellent facilities, including a network of fiber-optic cables, and benefits from partnerships with Microsoft and IBM. 4 years
BS INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY Silliman University offers a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT) program, which is part of its College of Computer Studies (CCS) and designated as a CHED Center of Excellence. The BSIT program focuses on practical, hands-on learning in areas like programming, database management, networking, and cybersecurity, preparing graduates for careers in software development, IT support, and more. 4 years
BS INFORMATION SYSTEMS Silliman University's Bachelor of Science in Information Systems (BSIS) is a four-year degree program within the College of Computer Studies (CCS) that focuses on designing, developing, and implementing computer-based information systems and other ICT solutions to address business process needs. The program emphasizes practical, hands-on learning through projects and assignments, preparing graduates for various roles such as system analysts, database administrators, and IT project managers in the evolving technology landscape. 4 years
BL INFORMATION SCIENCES Silliman University's Bachelor of Library and Information Science (BLIS) is a four-year degree program under the College of Computer Studies that prepares students for careers in managing and developing information resources in various formats, including digital and electronic. The program equips graduates with the skills to work in diverse information-related fields, such as academic, public, and corporate libraries, archives, and other information centers. 4 years
Graduate Programs
MASTER IN INFORMATION SYSTEMS Silliman University's Master in Information Systems (MIS) is a non-thesis graduate program designed to prepare students for industrial practice in managing information systems, including project management, planning, design, development, and managing technical staff. It focuses on developing students' skills in project management and information systems design to help companies manage technical personnel and solve business challenges through the effective use of information and communication technologies. 2 years
